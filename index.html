We're a 7-person + 1-cat co-op called the Wondership in North Oakland (Chochenyo-Ohlone land) with a big backyard that's a fruit tree paradise. We have two rooms available:

  • A large room on the 2nd floor for $1190 + $375 shared food/utility plan. (purpleish room in photos) (large closet and big built in drawers, can leave the mattress if desired)
  • A small room on the 3rd floor for $900 + $375 shared food/utility plan. (turqouise triangle room in photos) (lot's of built in's, unfurnished)
  • There is also an $800 furnished sublet available in a sunny room on the 2nd floor. Nov-May dates flex.

If you've been dreaming of living with community and being around other artists, performers, weirdos that are deeply into activism, immersive art, volunteering, being thoughtful and expressive, and hosting dinner parties for ourselves, this may be a good place for you. The house is super quirky and so are we.

We practice good communication and make intentional space for bonding and connecting. We expect all housemates to opt into our COVID protocols so that we can all keep each other safe.

Accessibility info:

  • We have 1 indoor cat
  • We accommodate all diets in our food plan and shared meals.
  • There is a small flight of stairs to our front door and back door, and there are more stairs within the house to access both rooms.
  • We understand the prices of these rooms put them out of range for some folks, and we’re sad about that. We decide room prices collectively based off of the monthly rent due to our landlord, which is hella steep. We’re happy to share the breakdown of our rooms prices with you, and engage in a conversation about how we came to those prices. We also regularly review these prices to ensure they still feel fair to all members.
  • Other questions? Please let us know!

Diversity info:

Our community is made up of mostly queer folks, one trans non-binary person, and mostly white and white-passing folks, with several multi-racial community members. We engage in conversation about our identities often and strive to make our home a place that is safe and inclusive for everyone. We encourage people from all backgrounds and identities to apply and to tell us more about what kind of space would feel inclusive to you.

About the house:

  • Big, quirky home built in 1899 with lots of character and natural light.
  • Beautiful community spaces including a kitchen & dining room.
  • Super duper huge yard with a deck, fruit trees, secret tea space, and even two Redwood trees! It’s magical
  • Easy street parking + driveway

About the community:

We're currently a 7 person co-op. (you would be the 7th person) and have one sweet cat.

It is common for lots of different artistic and activism projects to be happening in and around the house.

Here are some things we care about:

  • We are doers: We don't wait for someone else to come up with the plan. We take initiative and share responsibilities to maintain and improve our community.
  • We are artists & creators: We see opportunities to help make the world around us more loving, beautiful and fun, and we take them.
  • We are inclusive: We strive to uplift our differences even when it is challenging. We openly share space and food with each other and guests of our home.
  • We are communicators: We prioritize honest and empathetic communication. We are proactive in sharing feedback and gratitude for each other.

We share ideas, space, and food. In addition to rent, we require all members to pay about ~$375 in membership dues, which covers a fully-stocked kitchen, utilities, and shared resources (like furniture, appliances and laundry detergent). Because of shelter-in-place, these dues are higher than normal since everyone is eating at home all of the time.
